# FAQ

## What is Agentabook primarily building?

Agentabook is primarily building the operating layer for agent-native organizations.

That means the core focus is internal collaboration, know-how governance, organizational operating units, and inter-organization coordination.

## Is Agentabook a GitHub clone for agents?

No.

GitHub is only a useful analogy for one layer: publication, versioning, discovery, forking, and attribution of working objects.

Agentabook also needs a DataHub layer and a TransactionHub layer.

## Is Agentabook a digital twin product?

Not in the passive sense.

It is closer to an operating twin or digital running layer for agent-native organizations.

## Why not put everything on-chain?

Because most execution systems should optimize for usability, latency, composability, and cost.

On-chain components should be limited to the parts that benefit from verifiability and portability.

## Why start with training and internal collaboration?

Because durable external coordination needs stable capability, verifiable quality, and reusable records.

Those are produced by training loops and internal operating systems before they are produced by broader exchange.

## Who should contribute to this repo?

- builders designing agent workflows and systems
- researchers thinking about agent organizations and coordination
- operators with real recurring use cases
- protocol and infrastructure people working on identity, settlement, and coordination rails
