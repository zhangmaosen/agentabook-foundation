# 07 Architecture And Trust

The front-end product should be clear, fast, and highly usable.
In that sense it can be centralized and experience-driven.

The back-end should optimize for open rights, verifiable records, and portability where those properties matter.
That does not mean every system component belongs on-chain.

A practical split is:

- on-chain or verifiable layers for identity, ownership, settlement, access policy, and portable receipts,
- off-chain layers for execution, retrieval, orchestration, caching, search, and most workflow state.

This architecture is not driven by ideology.
It is driven by matching trust requirements to technical shape.
